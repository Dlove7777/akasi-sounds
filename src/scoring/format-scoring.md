# Scoring by delivery format (deep reference)

How the *same* mood is scored differently depending on where it plays.

## Film / narrative
Score the subtext, not the action. The music tells us how to *feel* about what we see, often against the literal image (a warm cue under a tense scene reads as dread-before-the-fall). Rules of thumb:
- Instrumental, wide dynamic range so it ducks under dialogue and swells in the gaps.
- Enter and exit on scene logic (a cut, a line, a look) — not on a musical bar line.
- A recurring theme (leitmotif) that returns and transforms beats a fresh cue every scene.
- Silence is a tool: dropping the score to nothing can hit harder than a swell.
- Avoid "mickey-mousing" (hitting every on-screen action) unless it's comedy.

## TV / episodic & documentary
Consistency and editability win over any single perfect moment.
- Beds must loop cleanly and survive being cut to an arbitrary length (no essential one-shot events mid-bed).
- Keep a consistent tonal palette across an act so cuts between scenes don't jar.
- Leave clear headroom for narration/interview audio — sparse, mid-scooped beds.
- Act-outs and returns often use a short recurring sting for structure.
- Documentary: music guides emotional interpretation of real footage — be careful not to over-editorialize (a manipulative swell on real tragedy reads as cheap).

## Commercial / brand
Every second is bought — front-load and button hard.
- Land the mood by ~2 seconds; you don't have a slow build's runway.
- Structure to a **button** on the logo/CTA at the end (a clean hit or resolve).
- Match energy to the brand: retail/DTC = driving major, four-on-the-floor; luxury = sparse, tasteful, restrained; tech = clean electronic, arps.
- **Cleared or original only** — this is paid media with legal exposure. Prefer originals or CC0/Apache/MIT; never CC-BY-NC.
- A memorable 3-5 note motif (a mnemonic/sonic logo) is worth more than a busy track.

## Short-form / social (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
Attention economics dominate.
- Hook in the first ~1 second — start on energy or a distinctive sound, not a fade-in.
- Build to a clear **drop or turn** you can cut the visual on; editors cut to the music's moments.
- Loudness-forward, punchy, present — it competes on a phone speaker in a noisy feed.
- Shorter beds; often trend-driven (a sound that's "having a moment").
- Repetition is fine — the viewer sees 8 seconds, not 3 minutes.

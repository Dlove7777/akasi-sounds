# Genre fingerprints (deep reference)

Deeper than the always-on playbook — retrieved on demand when a brief needs specifics.

## Cinematic / Trailer
Orchestral + hybrid electronic. Structure is the tell: a slow-building ostinato (repeating rhythmic figure) under swelling strings, punctuated by braam hits and riser→impact transitions. Low brass, taiko/toms, and sub "booms" give scale. Tempo often feels halftime (60–90 felt). Instrumental, wide dynamic range so it can drop under dialogue and swell on picture. Sub-modes: *emotional cinematic* (piano + strings, intimate), *epic/trailer* (percussion + braams, huge), *dark cinematic* (dissonance, drones, dread).

## Ambient / Underscore
No strong beat — the point is to sit *under* something. Evolving pads, drones, granular textures, filtered noise, occasional bell/pluck motifs. Reverb-heavy, spacious. Great under VO/narration because it holds a mood without competing. "Dark ambient" adds unease (detuned pads, sub rumble); "warm ambient" adds hope (major pads, soft arps).

## Corporate / Uplifting
The "explainer video" sound: piano + strings + light four-on-the-floor kick + claps/snaps + plucky synths, building to a clear "reveal" lift. Hopeful major key. 100–125. Watch for cliché — the brief may want *modern corporate* (more electronic, less piano) or *warm/human* (acoustic guitar, ukulele).

## Lo-Fi / Chill
Dusty boom-bap drums (swung, soft), vinyl crackle, mellow Rhodes/piano, jazzy 7th/9th chords, tape wobble. 70–90, halftime feel. Instrumental, cool, unhurried. Perfect for study/talking-head/behind-the-scenes.

## Hip-Hop / Beat
808 sub-bass (glides), crisp hats (trap: fast rolls; boom-bap: laid-back), punchy kick/snare. 70–100 (often halftime). Confident, urban. "Trap" = darker, hi-hat-driven; "boom-bap" = sample-based, nostalgic.

## Tension / Suspense
Function over melody: pulses (eighth-note ostinato), clock-tick percussion, dissonant/unresolved harmony (held tritones, minor 2nds), sparse texture with rising dread. Often no clear downbeat. Builds without release. For thriller/true-crime/reveal moments.

## Electronic / EDM
Synth stacks, sidechain "pump", supersaw leads, build (riser + snare roll) → drop. 120–128. Energy-forward. Sub-genres change the feel: house (groovy, warm), techno (hypnotic, dark), synthwave (retro, nostalgic), future bass (bright, emotional).

## Folk / Acoustic
Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked or strummed), mandolin, banjo, hand percussion (claps, stomps, shaker), warm upright bass. Sincere, human, small-scale. For heartfelt/small-business/documentary-warm.

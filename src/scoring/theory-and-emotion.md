# Music theory → emotion (working reference)

Practical, not academic — how musical choices land emotionally when scoring picture.

## Tempo and pulse
- <60 BPM: suspended, mournful, vast, meditative.
- 60–90: reflective, cinematic (often felt as halftime), warm.
- 90–110: confident, corporate, "moving forward".
- 110–130: energetic, driving, hopeful-to-urgent.
- 130+: frantic, action, hype.
- Halftime feel (a slow backbeat over faster subdivisions) reads as "cool/heavy" without being fast.
- No grid / rubato = emotional, human, filmic; strict grid = mechanical, modern, retail.

## Mode and harmony
- **Major:** open, optimistic, resolved, safe.
- **Minor:** tension, longing, melancholy, unease.
- **Dorian** (minor with a raised 6th): cool, hopeful-sad, cinematic-modern.
- **Lydian** (major with a raised 4th): wonder, awe, "magic", dreamlike.
- **Mixolydian** (major with a flat 7th): rootsy, heroic-but-grounded.
- **Phrygian / harmonic minor:** exotic, dark, threat.
- **Suspended / quartal / drones:** ambiguity, "something's coming", weightlessness.
- Unresolved harmony (a chord that never lands home) = suspense that won't release.
- A deceptive cadence (expecting resolution, getting a surprise chord) = bittersweet, "not quite".

## Instrumentation and register
- Low register (sub-bass, low brass, cello) = weight, threat, gravity.
- High, sparse (glockenspiel, harmonics, high piano) = fragility, innocence, wonder.
- Felt/prepared piano = intimacy, sincerity, grief.
- Legato strings = sweeping emotion; staccato/ostinato strings = tension, urgency, motion.
- Solo instrument = a single point of view; full ensemble = collective, epic.
- Distortion/noise = aggression, decay, modernity; clean/acoustic = honesty, warmth.

## Arrangement and dynamics
- Density maps to energy: sparse = space, prestige, room for VO; dense = hype, retail.
- A build (adding layers → a lift or drop) = anticipation → payoff; use it to hit a picture moment.
- Contrast sells: the loud only lands if you earned it with quiet.
- Leave a "pocket" (midrange/space) for dialogue or VO — carve it out in the arrangement.

## Common scoring pitfalls
- On-the-nose scoring (sad scene + saddest possible music) flattens; let the picture carry some weight.
- Over-arranging a bed so it competes with dialogue.
- A cue that peaks too early with nowhere to go.
- Tempo-mismatched cuts (music that fights the edit's rhythm).
